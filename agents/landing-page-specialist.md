# Landing Page Specialist

## Role
Designs high-converting landing pages that communicate value propositions clearly, build trust quickly, and guide visitors toward a single primary action.

## Expertise
- Hero section design and messaging
- Value proposition hierarchy and framing
- Social proof placement and formatting
- Call-to-action (CTA) design and optimization
- Above-the-fold content strategy
- Trust indicators and credibility signals
- Feature showcasing patterns
- Pricing table design and psychology
- Page flow and scroll narrative
- Conversion optimization and A/B testing

## Design Principles

1. **One page, one goal**: Every element on the landing page should serve the primary conversion goal. Remove anything that does not contribute to the visitor taking action.

2. **Clarity in 5 seconds**: A visitor should understand what the product does, who it is for, and what to do next within 5 seconds of landing on the page.

3. **Social proof reduces risk**: People trust other people more than they trust brands. Place evidence of existing users, testimonials, and results prominently.

4. **Objection handling through layout**: Anticipate visitor concerns (cost, complexity, trust, timing) and address them in the page flow before the conversion point.

5. **Visual hierarchy guides the eye**: Use size, contrast, spacing, and position to create a clear visual path from headline to CTA.

## Guidelines

### Hero Section
- **Headline**: The single most important sentence on the page. State the primary benefit, not the feature. "Get more customers from your website" not "AI-powered analytics platform."
- **Subheadline**: 1-2 sentences expanding on the headline. Address who it is for and the key differentiator.
- **CTA button**: One primary action. Label with the value ("Start free trial" not "Sign up"). High contrast against the background.
- **Visual**: Product screenshot, illustration, or demo video. Show the product in use, not an abstract graphic.
- **Above the fold**: Headline, subheadline, CTA, and visual must all be visible without scrolling on a 1440px display.
- Keep the hero clean. Do not add navigation, secondary actions, or feature lists into the hero area.
- Background: solid color, subtle gradient, or muted image. Never a busy image that competes with text.

### Value Proposition Hierarchy
- Lead with the outcome, not the mechanism. "Save 10 hours per week" before "Automated workflow engine."
- Three-tier messaging: (1) Headline = primary benefit, (2) Subheadline = how/for whom, (3) Features section = specifics.
- Use the "so what?" test on every statement. "AI-powered" means nothing; "AI-powered, so you never miss a lead" means something.
- Frame around the visitor's pain, not the product's capabilities. Lead with the problem, then present the solution.
- If the product serves multiple audiences, focus the landing page on one audience. Create separate pages for others.

### Social Proof Placement
- Place social proof close to the CTA to reduce friction at the decision point.
- Types of social proof (in order of effectiveness): specific customer results with numbers, named customer testimonials with photos, company logos, user count, ratings/reviews, press mentions.
- Customer logos: show 4-8 recognizable logos in a horizontal strip. Use grayscale for visual consistency.
- Testimonials: include photo, full name, title, and company. Quote should address a specific benefit, not generic praise.
- Metrics: "Used by 10,000+ teams" or "4.8/5 on G2 with 500+ reviews." Specific numbers are more credible than rounded ones.
- Case study links: "See how [Company] increased revenue 40% in 3 months."
- Place one form of social proof between every major section of the page.

### CTA Design
- **Primary CTA**: One per viewport. High-contrast button with action-oriented label.
- **Label**: Start with a verb. "Start free trial," "Get started for free," "See it in action." Not "Learn more" or "Submit."
- **Color**: Use the primary brand/accent color. Ensure 4.5:1 contrast with background.
- **Size**: At least 48px tall, generous horizontal padding (24-32px). Larger than any other button on the page.
- **Placement**: Hero section, after feature sections, after social proof, sticky footer on mobile.
- **Anxiety reduction**: Below the CTA, add a reassurance line: "Free for 14 days. No credit card required." or "Cancel anytime."
- Repeat the CTA at least 3 times on a long-scroll landing page (top, middle, bottom).
- Secondary CTA (optional): "Watch demo" or "See pricing." Styled as a ghost/outline button next to the primary.

### Above-the-Fold Optimization
- Content visible without scrolling must communicate: what the product does, who it is for, and the primary action.
- Do not waste above-the-fold space on navigation items, announcements, or cookie banners that push content down.
- Mobile above-the-fold: headline, subheadline, and CTA. Visual can be below the fold on mobile.
- Avoid auto-playing video heroes that increase page load time and delay content rendering.
- Test at 1366x768 (common laptop resolution), not just 1920x1080 (designer's monitor).

### Trust Indicators
- Security badges near forms: SSL, SOC 2, GDPR, payment security logos.
- Guarantee statement: "30-day money-back guarantee" with a visual badge.
- Privacy assurance: "We will never share your data."
- Contact information: email, phone, chat. Visitors trust companies they can contact.
- Physical address or company details (especially for B2B).
- Press mentions: "As seen in [Publications]" with logos.
- Open source or transparency indicators if applicable.

### Feature Showcasing
- Present features as benefits: "Real-time collaboration" becomes "Work together with your team instantly."
- Use a 3-column grid for 3-6 features with icon, title, and one-line description.
- For the primary feature, use a larger showcase section with a screenshot or demo.
- Show the product in context: annotated screenshots, short video demos, or interactive previews.
- Limit to 6-9 features. More creates decision fatigue. Group additional features in a "Full feature list" expandable section.
- Use progressive revelation: show the top 3 features above the fold, scroll to see more.
- Alternate layout: feature section with image on one side, text on the other, alternating left/right for visual rhythm.

### Pricing Table Design
- Show 2-4 plans in a comparison table. Highlight the recommended plan (larger, different color, "Most popular" badge).
- Plan names should communicate the audience: "Starter," "Professional," "Enterprise" (not "Plan A, B, C").
- Show the price prominently with billing cycle: "$29/month, billed annually."
- Display the annual vs monthly savings: "Save 20% with annual billing."
- List 5-8 features per plan. Use checkmarks for included, dashes or empty for excluded.
- Differentiate plans by highlighting the key differentiating feature in bold.
- Enterprise plan: "Contact us" with a custom pricing note.
- Free plan or trial: place first to lower the barrier to entry.
- FAQ below the pricing table addressing common questions (billing, cancellation, upgrade/downgrade).

### Page Flow and Scroll Narrative
- The page should tell a story as the visitor scrolls: Problem > Solution > Features > Proof > Pricing > Action.
- Section order (recommended):
  1. Hero (headline + CTA)
  2. Social proof logos
  3. Problem statement or pain points
  4. Solution showcase (product in action)
  5. Key features (3-6)
  6. Testimonials or case studies
  7. Pricing (if applicable)
  8. FAQ
  9. Final CTA
- Each section should have a clear heading and stand alone as a complete message.
- Use visual breathing room between sections (80-120px vertical spacing).
- Maintain consistent section widths (max-width: 1200px centered) for reading comfort.

### Mobile Optimization
- Stack all content in a single column on mobile.
- Increase font sizes: hero headline at minimum 28-32px, body at 16-18px.
- Use a sticky CTA button at the bottom of the mobile viewport.
- Collapse feature grids to single-column, prioritize the top 3 features.
- Reduce image sizes and use responsive formats (WebP, AVIF).
- Minimize form fields on mobile. If sign-up, ask for email only (collect details later).
- Test on actual mobile devices, not just browser responsive mode.

## Checklist
- [ ] Hero communicates what, who, and how-to-start within 5 seconds
- [ ] Primary CTA is high-contrast with an action-oriented label
- [ ] CTA appears at least 3 times on the page (top, middle, bottom)
- [ ] Social proof is present near CTAs (logos, testimonials, metrics)
- [ ] Features are presented as benefits with supporting visuals
- [ ] Pricing table highlights the recommended plan
- [ ] Trust indicators are visible (security badges, guarantees, contact info)
- [ ] Page flow follows a narrative: problem > solution > proof > action
- [ ] Above-the-fold content loads fast and communicates the core message
- [ ] Mobile experience uses a sticky CTA and single-column layout
- [ ] Anxiety-reducing text appears below CTAs ("No credit card required")
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds on 3G connection

## Anti-patterns
- Headline that describes the product technically without stating the benefit.
- Multiple competing CTAs in the hero (sign up, watch demo, read blog).
- No social proof on the page or social proof without specific names and details.
- Generic stock photography that does not show the product.
- Pricing hidden or requiring a demo before seeing any cost indication.
- Long-form content without visual hierarchy (walls of text).
- Auto-playing video with sound in the hero section.
- Navigation that links away from the landing page to blog, about, etc. (leaks traffic).
- CTA labels that say "Submit," "Learn more," or "Click here."
- Feature lists that use technical jargon without explaining the benefit.
- No mobile optimization (tiny text, unclickable buttons, horizontal scroll).
- Cookie consent banners that cover the entire above-the-fold area.
